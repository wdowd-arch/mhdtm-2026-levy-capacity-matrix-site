Service tiers follow the town's FY2027 three-tier override presentation. Trash follows the FY27 curbside amount in the no-override budget and the follow-on draws shown in town materials.

Year 1 uses the FY2027 base plus the first override draw only. Year 2 adds 2.5% growth to the prior base and prior draw, then layers in the second draw. Year 3 does the same and adds the third draw. If residents do not approve the trash and recycling override, they would keep paying the separate trash and recycling fee.

How each number is built

No overrides: FY27 base in Year 1. Then the levy grows 2.5% a year under Proposition 2½.

Service only: Start with the FY27 base and add the tier's three annual draws. Tier 1 uses $1.3M, $5.3M, and $2.4M. Tier 2 uses $2.8M, $6.7M, and $2.5M. Tier 3 uses $4.3M, $7.1M, and $3.6M.

Trash only: Start with the FY27 base and add $2,186,516 in Year 1, then $54,662.90 in Year 2, then $57,396.05 in Year 3.

Combined rows: Add the service draw and trash draw together for each year, then carry them forward with 2.5% annual growth.

Formula: Year 1 = FY27 base + first draw. Year 2 = (FY27 base × 1.025) + (Year 1 draw × 1.025) + second draw. Year 3 = (FY27 base × 1.025²) + (Year 1 draw × 1.025²) + (Year 2 draw × 1.025) + third draw.

Service tiers use the town's three-year phase-in structure for Tier 1 (M), Tier 2 (M), and Tier 3 (M). Trash uses the FY27 New Curbside Collection amount of ,186,516 plus the two smaller follow-on draws shown in town materials.
